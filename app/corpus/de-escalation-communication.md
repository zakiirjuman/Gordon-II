title: De-escalation communication — calm command presence
category: authority
source: Crisis communication and police de-escalation training summary, not legal advice
tags: de-escalation, communication, calm, distance, time, crisis
---

When safe, slow the encounter: create distance, reduce audience pressure, use one clear voice, and explain what you need the person to do next.

Calm command presence is not weakness. It preserves officer control by reducing surprise, narrowing choices, and giving backup time to arrive.

Avoid sarcasm, threats, or repeated overlapping commands when a person is already dysregulated. Document safety concerns and the steps taken to reduce risk.
