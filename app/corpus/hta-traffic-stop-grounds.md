A motor vehicle stop requires lawful authority: typically a statutory power under the Highway Traffic Act (equipment, licensing, speeding, etc.) or reasonable grounds to investigate an offence.

Random stops without statutory basis are not lawful. Articulate the specific reason before or immediately after the stop (e.g., observed defect, information plus corroboration, radar/lidar where authorized).

Stops near active construction or lane closures may affect sight lines and traffic flow — position the vehicle for officer and public safety before engagement.
